# Ancient-tech surface kit

> **Status:** build (2026-07-22)

## Context

A six-tile seamless environment-texture family for the **robotic** domain's *ancient-tech* flavour ‚Äî
the monumental cyan-on-graphite precursor architecture, as distinct from the domain's existing
industrial-armour lane (`exterior-industrial-armor-wall-01`, `depot-grate-floor-02`). Two coherent
sub-sets: a vertically-stacked exterior **wall** ladder (crown ¬∑ field ¬∑ plinth) and a **transit**
ground set (road ¬∑ sidewalk ¬∑ plaza).

Generated by Codex outside the `generation/BATCH.md` contract ‚Äî a third unlogged Codex island after
the `roblox-textured-ui-ux` thread (surfaced in the 2026-07-21 review). `BATCH.md` held, and still
holds, `roguex-33-portrait`; this run never displaced it. This entity exists to put the run on the
graph retroactively: the art is done and verified, filing is not.

The family shares its palette with the `graphite-cyan` UI style kit
(`Assets/templates/ui-style/graphite-cyan/`, from [[roblox-textured-ui-reference-kit]]) ‚Äî the
environment counterpart of that specimen set, though the two were briefed independently.

## Source specification

Six 2048√ó2048 opaque PNGs, all **verified 4-way seamless** ‚Äî wrap-edge delta measured against
in-image local delta; every tile lands within noise (H 0.87‚Äì1.12√ó, V 1.09‚Äì1.58√ó; the elevated wall
V-ratios are the banded design crossing its own wrap, not a seam). Codex also shipped `qa-repeat-*`
2√ó2 proofs and two family previews; those are **review evidence, not library assets** ‚Äî they stay
out of `textures/` and remain with this record.

Domain is `robotic` per the taxonomy ("tech, mechanical, cyber, hard-surface"); the `ancient-tech`
family token is borrowed from the already-registered
`templates/key-art/character-promo-robotic-ancient-tech-theme.png` rather than improvised. The
`exterior-` placement prefix follows the 2026-06-11 domain-wall set and applies to walls only ‚Äî
ground surfaces carry no placement token (`cursed-slate-ground`, `null-slate-floor-01`).

**Face-dependent V mapping** ‚Äî the rule that decides what "one tile" means for a band.
`StudsPerTileV` maps to a different part axis per face: Front/Back and Left/Right ‚Üí part **Y**;
Top/Bottom ‚Üí part **Z**. A crown on a wall face takes `V = Size.Y`; the same texture on a ceiling
face takes `V = Size.Z`. Getting this wrong is exactly what turns a band into a repeating stripe.

`walkway` ‚Üí `plaza` on the owner's call: walkway/sidewalk are near-synonyms and the image is a wide
chamfered-slab concourse, not a footpath.

**Runtime resolution: 1024¬≤** (owner call, 2026-07-22). Roblox caps uploaded textures at 1024¬≤, so
2048 ships bytes the platform discards; the downsample was re-seam-checked and holds. The 2048
masters archive to `textures/tileable/robotic/_archive/2026-07-22/<name>.original-2048x2048.png`,
the resize-provenance pattern `textures/skybox/soul-steel-01/` already uses.

**Consumption:** `Texture` instances with `StudsPerTileU/V` ‚Äî not `SurfaceAppearance` (MeshPart-only)
and not `Decal` (doesn't tile). Two standing gotchas, carried into every sidecar:

- **Crown and plinth are bands, not fields.** Build walls as three parts, each band's
  `StudsPerTileV` set to that part's height so it renders exactly once vertically. Tiled 4√ó vertically
  they produce a repeating crown stripe up the wall.
- **Baked top-down lighting** on the crown/plinth bevels locks them upright. Correct for walls in
  fixed orientation; do not mirror or rotate them.

The studs-per-tile column is **resolved** ‚Äî swept empirically on the authored 2/4/8/12-stud rig in
the Part-Texture-Testing-Lab (run-2), not estimated. Paver density differs sharply across the set,
so the family shares one declared scale reference to stay consistent where surfaces meet.

## Open threads

- **No curb tile.** The brief called for roads ¬∑ curbs ¬∑ sidewalks; Codex produced road, sidewalk and
  walkway. A curb is the ground equivalent of the wall plinth ‚Äî a directional trim band ‚Äî and needs a
  follow-up batch, this time through `generation/BATCH.md`.
- **Three more Codex batches landed 2026-07-22 (11:56‚Äì12:34), unreviewed and unfiled.** Same
  ancient-tech visual language, but a **different asset class** ‚Äî 3840√ó2160 concept/reference
  artboards, not tileable textures, so none of this record's naming, resolution or `Texture` config
  applies to them:
  - `output/imagegen/ancient-alien-architectural-openings/` ‚Äî a 3√ó3 board of in-wall openings
    (windows ¬∑ doors ¬∑ passageways √ó three size tiers).
  - `output/imagegen/ancient-alien-portal-mechanisms/` ‚Äî a 3√ó3 board of nontraditional freestanding
    apertures (iris ¬∑ triangular ¬∑ hexagonal ¬∑ mandorla ¬∑ spiral ¬∑ lens ¬∑ octagonal ¬∑ cruciform ¬∑ C-ring).
  - `output/imagegen/ancient-alien-portal-material-study/` ‚Äî a portal frame + iris-blade material study.

  These are the *design* layer above the surface kit and read as `concept-art/` in the library (by
  collection), not `textures/`. They may instead qualify as generated exemplars under
  [[0010-templates-split-by-authorship-exemplars-get-a-library-templa]] if the intent is to generate
  *from* them ‚Äî that is an owner
  call, and it decides the destination. They also arrived outside `generation/BATCH.md`, same as
  run-1. Recommend a `concept-art` sibling pipeline rather than stretching this one, since nothing
  about the tileable contract transfers.
- **Tileable budget drift.** The sidecar acceptance template says "512√ó512, under 500 KB"; these are
  1024¬≤ at ~1.6 MB. 512 would fit (394 KB) but loses the crown/plinth glyph detail. The budget line
  needs amending to a size-tiered rule, or these need an explicit exemption.
- **No normal maps.** `generic-panel-01-normal_BLK.png` sets a precedent for the robotic domain;
  these are colour-only, with lighting baked into the crown/plinth relief.
- **The road cannot be rotated in-texture.** `Texture` has no rotation property, so a road running
  along world X needs its part rotated 90¬∞ about Y ‚Äî or a pre-rotated `-02` variant authored. Worth
  deciding before the kit gets used for a street grid.
- **The lab package now carries unpublished local changes.** Deliberately not published ‚Äî see
  "Package state" below.
- **`TrembusGameLibrary` as the non-TS path** (owner note, 2026-07-22). The lab is itself a Roblox
  Package, which is the shape this would take: two delivery paths kept in parallel ‚Äî the roblox-ts
  path and a Package path ‚Äî with Roblox's own Package versioning standing in for git/backup on the
  non-TS side. Not scoped here; wants its own session and probably an ADR, since it is a second
  distribution channel for the same assets and the two paths can drift.

## Verification evidence

Wrap-seam check at source resolution ‚Äî mean abs RGB delta across the wrap edge vs the in-image
adjacent-pair baseline; ratio near 1.0 means the seam is indistinguishable from ordinary local
variation:

| Tile | H seam / base | V seam / base |
|---|---:|---:|
| `wall-top-crown` | 5.64 / 6.51 = 0.87√ó | 7.28 / 5.66 = 1.29√ó |
| `wall-mid-main` | 3.98 / 3.56 = 1.12√ó | 5.30 / 3.46 = 1.53√ó |
| `wall-bottom-plinth` | 6.19 / 5.94 = 1.04√ó | 8.58 / 5.42 = 1.58√ó |
| `road` | 6.39 / 6.53 = 0.98√ó | 5.19 / 4.76 = 1.09√ó |
| `sidewalk` | 6.03 / 6.86 = 0.88√ó | 7.05 / 8.03 = 0.88√ó |
| `walkway` (‚Üí plaza) | 6.28 / 7.08 = 0.89√ó | 9.11 / 7.54 = 1.21√ó |

Downsample integrity, `wall-top-crown` via `sips -Z`: 1024¬≤ ‚Üí 1.6 MB, H 0.99√ó / V 1.54√ó;
512¬≤ ‚Üí 394 KB, H 0.95√ó / V 1.78√ó. Seamlessness survives both; 1024 selected. Re-checked after
filing: all six filed 1024s hold (H 0.90‚Äì1.71√ó, V 1.04‚Äì1.84√ó).

### Studio pass ‚Äî run-2, 2026-07-22

Place: **Soul Steel** (`PlaceId 102596975485791`), Edit mode, `Workspace.Part-Texture-Testing-Lab`
(a Package, 1135 descendants, 4 sub-labs). Local content resolved through the `rbxasset://trembus/`
symlink ‚Äî relinked earlier the same day, which also clears the blocker
[[roblox-textured-ui-reference-kit]] has been parked on since 2026-07-12.

- **Load check:** all six URIs pass `ContentProvider:PreloadAsync` and render.
- **Slots dressed:** 79 total ‚Äî 35 in `PartTextureTestLab` (scale sweep + environment zones),
  44 in `PartTextureWallAssemblyLab` (both bays). Empty slots went 34 ‚Üí **0**. Two
  `ChangeHistoryService` recordings, so the whole pass is two undos.
- **Scale sweep** on the rig's authored 2/4/8/12-stud slots ‚Äî floor grid rows R1/R2/R3 =
  road/sidewalk/plaza, `WallPanel_01..04` = field. Findings: the family wants the **coarse** end.
  Field collapses to noise at 2‚Äì4, reads at 8, best at 12. Sidewalk best at 8 ‚Äî at 2 the pavers
  vanish into flat concrete, at 12 they compete with the plaza. Plaza and road best at 12.
  12 was the top of the authored sweep, so 16 remains untested.
- **Band rule confirmed both ways.** `ControlledBay` (crown at `V=10` on the 10-stud upper band,
  plinth at `V=4` on the 4-stud lower band) renders each band exactly once and reads as intended
  monumental architecture. The `CeilingPanel` row is the negative control: crown swept at 2/4/8/12
  on a 17-stud face repeats as a stripe.
- **A/B, both bays:** `ControlledBay` = plinth + crown (the designed assembly), `ContextBay` =
  plinth + plain field. The crowned assembly wins clearly; the plain field wall reads flat.
- **Road direction confirmed:** lane channels run along the part's local **Z** on a Top face.
- **Rig gotcha:** `WallPanel_*` parts carry `Orientation (0, 180, 0)`, so their `Front` face points
  world **+Z**. Cost one mis-framed capture; worth knowing before scripting camera work here.
- **Lighting caveat:** judged under the place's default outdoor `Lighting`, which washes this very
  dark palette. The lab's own `LightingRigs` folder was not exercised ‚Äî a pass under those (and
  under night/interior settings) would be a fairer read of the cyan emissive traces.
